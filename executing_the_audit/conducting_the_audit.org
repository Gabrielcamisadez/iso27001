#+title: Conducting The Audit
#+author:gabrie

Conducting the audit is the primary activity for assessing the conformity of a management system to an ISO standard

- conducting interviews, collecting and inspecting evidence, and observing walkthroughs

*Outputs*
- nonconformities, opportunities for improvement, positive findings, conformity determinations, and potentially certification

*lead auditor*
- oversees the entire audit process, manages timelines, approves findings, and ensures evidence appropriateness

*auditor*
- conducts interviews, reviews evidence, and identifies potential gaps

*technical expert*
- provides specialized knowledge (e.g., for technical controls or language translation) to ensure thorough and accurate audits

* phase 1 - preparation & planning
- includes the application process, building the audit program, and developing the audit plan

* phase 2 - collecting evidence
- gather evidence throuhg interviews, document reviews and process observations

* phase 3 - analysis & evaluation
- review collected evidence to determine conformity based on ISO requirements

* phase 4 - Findings & feedback
- resulting from the analysis, auditors will document their findings(both positive and negative) they have for continual improvement of the management system
