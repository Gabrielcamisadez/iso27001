# Accreditation vs Certification

**Accreditation** ->
Validates that a certification body is competent to perform certification audits. Accreditation evaluates a CB&rsquo;s competency to initially and continually conduct certification audits

ISO/IEC 17021-1: Requirements for management system CBs, ABs ensure CBs meet international standarts and marketplace expectations

**Certification** ->
Validates that an organization meets the requirements of a specific ISO standard. Certification involves evaluating an organization conformity to an ISO standart

Certification bodies assess wheter an organization management system conforms with the specific ISO standard. Certification often fulfills regulatory or customer requirements

-   Accreditation is voluntary for CBs to demonstrate credibility

-   Certification may be voluntary or mandatory, depending on regulatory, contractual, or customer reequirements

ISO/IEC 17021-1 defines requirements for certification bodies to achive accreditation

-   Auditor competency and audit conduct

-   Organizational processes and audit evidence management

-   Consistency in audit practices and time allocation

Certification bodies are audited annually to ensure ongoing compliance
